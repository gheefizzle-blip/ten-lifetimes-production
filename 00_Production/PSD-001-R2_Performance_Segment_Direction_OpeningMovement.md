## Director's ruling (verbatim)
> The failure of the first PS001 pass was not that Veo cannot make useful motion. The failure was that we asked one clip to carry too many jobs at once: walking, camera movement, emotional shift, pendant trigger, face continuity, Jen reveal, and recognition. The approach fixes that by making each clip responsible for one clear piece of storytelling.
>
> **We should rebuild PS001 as a three-clip edited sequence, not a single generated shot.** Total target remains 12.6 seconds: Clip 1 Observe 4.2 s · Clip 2 Trigger 4.2 s · Clip 3 Recognition 4.2 s. This preserves the locked timing while making the execution manageable.
>
> **The Performance Segment is the story unit. The motion clip is the execution unit. Those are not the same thing.**

## Clip 1 — OBSERVE (0.0–4.2 s)
- **Editorial purpose:** Establish isolation and scale.
- **Camera:** Elevated witness camera, approximately **50 feet high and 50 meters behind Gary**, moving forward and descending until roughly **2 meters behind him**. *It should feel like history itself is following him, not like a drone shot.*
- **Direction:** Gary walks alone along the **Nile riverbank** (not a beach). The camera slowly closes the distance without drama.
- **Performance:** Gary is unaware of anything unusual. He is not searching. His walk is routine.
- **End state:** Camera has moved into a close trailing position behind Gary.

## Clip 2 — TRIGGER (4.2–8.4 s)
- **Editorial purpose:** The ordinary world breaks by one tiny detail.
- **Camera:** Cut to a **front / off-axis view of Gary.** He is not looking into the camera; he is looking past it toward the river and the pale dawn beyond.
- **Direction:** The sun rises softly over the **distant desert hills** across the Nile (natural, not epic). A thin ray of light catches the **silver pendant.**
- **Performance:** Gary's eyes shift first. The reflection interrupts his routine. His expression changes almost imperceptibly. **He does not smile yet.** He senses something before he understands it.
- **End state:** Gary has looked forward and seen Jen for the first time; the beginning of recognition is visible in his eyes.

## Clip 3 — RECOGNITION (8.4–12.6 s)
- **Editorial purpose:** Reveal that the recognition is mutual, but asymmetrical.
- **Camera:** Cut to Jen entering the frame naturally. Prefer an **over-Gary-shoulder or slightly offset angle** — preserve connection without making it a staged romantic shot.
- **Direction:** Jen sees Gary. Her face brightens slightly — not a full smile yet, the smallest recognition. Her eyes say *"I know you"* before her mind understands why.
- **Performance:** **Gary led the recognition; Jen answers it.** Her smile begins as recognition in the **eyes, not mouth-first.** The full bloom belongs to HERO-04.
- **End state:** Arrive at the emotional state of HERO-03 and set up HERO-04.
