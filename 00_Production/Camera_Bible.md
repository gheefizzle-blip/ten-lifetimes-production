# TEN LIFETIMES — Camera Bible (working reference)

> Lens & movement language. **Canonical principle in `01_Production_Bible.md` §9 (the Emotional Wave).** This expands it into a usable grammar.

## Core rule
Camera scale follows the song's emotional intensity. **Never** move dramatically during intimate lyrics; grow visual scale as the orchestra grows.

## Narrative Camera language (PRIMARY — per Director, 2026-06-25)
The camera is described first by **what the audience should feel**, not by a focal length. Lens and movement are the *means*; the narrative register is the *intent*. Choose the register from the emotional beat, then pick lens/movement to serve it.

| Register | What it does | Audience feels | Typical means (secondary) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intimate** | Holds on the two of them; the world falls away | "Just them — I'm close enough to touch this" | 85mm, slow push, shallow DOF |
| **Observational** | Lets a moment breathe; we watch, unhurried | "I'm present in this life with them" | 50mm, static / slow drift |
| **Epic** | Opens to era and scale; love against history | "This is vast — and they're at the center of it" | 35mm / anamorphic, crane, rise |
| **Transcendent** | Leaves physical space; dream / cosmic | "Time and place are dissolving" | wide, gravity-off float (the Bridge) |
| **Personal** | Collapses fiction to reality; the real photo | "This was real all along" | 85mm, static hold, slow zoom only |

> The **Master Production Timeline** "Camera" column should read in this language first (e.g. *Intimate → Epic*), with lens/movement as a sub-note. The lens is secondary.

### Presence — the audience's relationship to the characters (per Director, 2026-06-25)
A deeper layer than movement or lens: **where is the audience *standing* emotionally?** Each Narrative-Camera register carries a Presence meaning. Direct the Presence; the lens follows.

| Register | **Presence — the audience…** |
|---|---|
| **Intimate** | …is standing **beside** them. |
| **Observational** | …**watches, unnoticed.** |
| **Epic** | …**witnesses history.** |
| **Transcendent** | …**leaves reality.** |
| **Personal** | …is **no longer watching — they're remembering.** |

> "Personal" is the finale's target: the moment the audience stops *watching a film* and starts *remembering a love*. The morph to the real photo (TS-010) is where Presence becomes Personal.

The **Emotional Wave mapping** below remains the scale-over-time backbone; the registers above are how each beat is *named*.

## Lens grammar
| Lens | Use | Feeling |
|---|---|---|
| 85mm | Emotional close-ups, the morph, the Bridge reunion | Intimacy, compression, "just them" |
| 50mm | Intimate two-shots, gestures | Natural, present |
| 35mm | Environmental wides, establishing | Place, scale, era |
| wide/anamorphic | Final-chorus convergence, cosmos | Epic, sweeping |

## Movement vocabulary
- **Slow push / dolly-in** — verses, intimacy, the morph to real photo.
- **Slow orbit** — gestures (let the silver pendant catch light).
- **Crane / rise** — chorus lifts, as strings swell.
- **Float / drift (gravity off)** — the Bridge (cosmic, dreamlike).
- **Static hold** — the final real photograph (slow zoom only).
- **Match-on-gesture / morph** — chapter-to-chapter transitions (Bible §10).

## Emotional Wave mapping
```
Intro    : fade-in, slow dolly (stars, gears)            — scale 1
Verse 1/2: 50–85mm, slow push, minimal move              — scale 2–4
Pre-chorus: begin widening as strings rise               — scale 5
Chorus 1/2: crane/rise, wide swell, impacts              — scale 6–8
Bridge   : drums out → intimate → cosmic float           — dip then surge
Final ch.: maximum scale, sweeping, convergence          — scale 10
Outro    : back to intimate push → morph → static hold   — scale 3 → land
```

## Standing rules
- 16:9 (2.39:1 letterbox optional). Default to motivated, single moves per shot (Sora handles one clean move best).
- Clip length ≤ tool max (Sora ≈ 20s). Design shots to that limit; prefer still→subtle-motion for hero faces (lowers artifact risk — Risk R-02).
