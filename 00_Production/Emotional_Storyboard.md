# TEN LIFETIMES — Emotional Storyboard

> **The layer between the Timeline and the Shot Database.** It answers **"What should the audience be *feeling*?"** — never "what is the camera doing." Story beats, not storyboards. (Per Director, 2026-06-25.)
>
> Hierarchy: Production Bible → Director's Timeline → **Master Production Timeline** → *Emotional Storyboard* → Shot Database. Every beat here maps to a measured time and a Shot ID; the camera/lens decisions live downstream in the Camera Bible and Shot DB.

- **Version:** v0.1 — Chapter One (Egypt) drafted by Agent B for **Director review**; S2–S9 stubbed.
- **Schema per beat:** `Time · Beat (what happens) · Emotion (one word) · Audience (what they feel) · **Memory** (what they remember 30 min after the film) · Transition (what carries us out)`
- **Memory field (per Director, 2026-06-25):** forces every beat to justify itself beyond the moment — *what lodges in the viewer's memory?* (e.g. 0:39.58 → "I knew this was the moment he finally found her.")
- **Maps to:** `MASTER_PRODUCTION_TIMELINE.md` (times/shots), `02_Directors_Timeline.md` §3 (section arcs).

## Chapter One — EGYPT (S1 · 0:19.02 – 0:48.84 · shots TL-0010–TL-0080)

**Chapter feeling, one line:** *Ten thousand years of looking for a face that never stayed — until one does.*
**Arc:** Loneliness across endless time → searching → the first spark of recognition.

| Time | Beat | Emotion | Audience feels | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:19.02 | "I've walked through empty centuries" — a soul alone in deep time | **Loneliness** | The weight of someone who has waited far too long | — |
| 0:22.18 | "Chasing ghosts through fading dreams" — reaching for faces that dissolve | **Yearning** | Hope that keeps slipping away | ghosts dissolve to dust |
| 0:26.26 | "Faces came and disappeared" — others pass, none remain | **Isolation** | The ache of near-misses | faces fade, one after another |
| 0:29.62 | "But none of them stayed here" — the loss lands | **Resignation** | Quiet grief; he's almost stopped believing | — |
| 0:33.24 | "Every road just led me back / to another lonely night" — the loop of solitude | **Weariness** | Sympathy — we've all had the long road home | the road empties into dusk |
| 0:39.58 | **"Till your smile cut through the dark"** — the turn; she appears | **Spark** | The first lift of the whole film — *there she is* | light breaks across the frame |
| 0:42.82 | "Like the first break of light" — recognition, dawn | **Hope** | "We've met before" — destiny clicking | golden flare → carries into Rome |

**Chapter-out (→ S2 Pre-Chorus / Rome):** the spark becomes certainty. The break-of-light flare is the hinge into **TS-001 (Egypt → Rome)** — see `Transition_Bible.md`.
