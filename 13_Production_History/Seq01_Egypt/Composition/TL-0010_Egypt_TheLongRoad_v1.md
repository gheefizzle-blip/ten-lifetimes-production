## Intent / Narrative Purpose
- **HERO-02 "The Long Road" (Director, CRE-003).** The audience has just witnessed possibility (HERO-01); now they must witness **persistence**. This is not a lonely man — a man who has lived with loneliness so long it has become ordinary. The tragedy is not that he is alone, but that he no longer expects that to change. The audience should not pity him; they should quietly begin walking beside him. **Narrative purpose:** establish Gary's emotional baseline before Jen enters the audience's awareness, so her later arrival feels like the answer to a question they didn't know they were asking. Lyric "I've walked through empty centuries..." (~0:19.020).

## Director Notes  (Director-authored — verbatim from CRE-003)
- Do not romanticize loneliness. Do not exaggerate sadness. Stillness is stronger than melancholy. The audience should feel that something is missing... without yet knowing what it is.

## THE LESSON
- Primary Category: Composition
- Secondary Categories: Camera Motion
- Severity: Major (misses the "accompanying / world larger than man" intent)
- Preventable?: PARTIALLY (the brief implied wide/observational; v1 rendered closer / profile-portrait)
- Reason for Archive: Camera too close; profile-portrait emphasis; Gary too large in frame — the audience studies his face instead of accompanying him.
- Root Cause: "three-quarter rear, modest in frame" plus 3 frontal face refs biased the model toward a near profile portrait that reads as studying him, not walking with him.
- Corrective Action: v2 pulled the camera far back; small + visibly-walking + fully back-to-camera figure; dropped face refs (ref-less) to avoid face-bias.
- Lessons Learned: For "accompanying / observational" beats, specify a WIDE / distant shot with a SMALL back-to-camera figure, and avoid conditioning on frontal face refs (they pull the subject closer / larger).
